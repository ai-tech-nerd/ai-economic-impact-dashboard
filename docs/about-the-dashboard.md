# AI Economic Impact Dashboard — About

## What it is

The AI Economic Impact Dashboard is a free, open-source, interactive data tool that tracks jobs cut or displaced due to artificial intelligence adoption by companies worldwide. The timeline begins November 30, 2022 — the day OpenAI launched ChatGPT — and is updated as new verified events are reported.

It is built as a static website hosted on GitHub Pages, making it permanently free to access with no sign-ups, paywalls, or ads.

## What it does

The dashboard brings together verified data, AI industry milestones, and actionable learning resources into six interactive views:

**Dashboard** — A real-time summary of total verified jobs displaced by AI, broken down by company, industry, job type, and trend over time. Animated counters, sortable tables, and charts make the scale immediately visible.

**Predictions** — Forward-looking estimates of which job categories face the highest risk of AI displacement over the next 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, and 3–5 years, with confidence ranges and methodology transparency.

**Timeline** — Two ways to experience the data over time. A slider mode lets you scrub through the timeline and watch displacement events accumulate. A story mode walks you through key chapters — from ChatGPT's explosive growth to 100 million users, through the first waves of AI-driven layoffs, to the current acceleration.

**AI Advancements** — A visual timeline of 207 major AI milestones: model releases (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama), company launches, acquisitions, partnerships and funding deals, regulatory actions across 12 countries, and technical breakthroughs. Filterable by type, company, and country.

**AI Companies** — Profiles of 27 AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, xAI, Midjourney, and more) with per-company milestone timelines, displacement data, and key stats.

**Learn & Prepare** — A practical resource hub adapted from "The AI Shift" guide. Includes free AI tools to start with, the CRAFT prompt framework, a 30-day action plan, skills AI cannot replace, and curated links to free courses from Anthropic, Google, DeepLearning.AI, IBM, NVIDIA, and Microsoft.

## Who it's for

**Workers and professionals** — People who want to understand how AI is reshaping their industry and what they can do to stay ahead. The dashboard makes abstract headlines concrete: real companies, real numbers, real timelines.

**Job seekers and career changers** — Anyone navigating a job market increasingly shaped by AI automation. The predictions and learning resources help them identify which roles are most at risk and which skills to develop.

**Journalists and researchers** — A verified, sourced dataset of AI displacement events and industry milestones. Every data point links to its original source — company statements, earnings calls, and credible reporting.

**Educators and trainers** — A teaching tool for workforce development programs, university courses, and corporate training. The timeline and story mode make the pace of change tangible in ways that reports and articles cannot.

**Business leaders and HR professionals** — Context for workforce planning decisions. The data shows not just who is cutting jobs, but which job types are most affected and how the trend is accelerating.

**Policy makers and advocates** — Evidence-based data on the scale and speed of AI-driven workforce displacement, useful for shaping policy, safety nets, and retraining programs.

## Why it's useful

**The data exists, but it's scattered.** AI displacement events are reported one headline at a time — a layoff here, an automation announcement there. No single source brings them together in a way that reveals the pattern. This dashboard does.

**Numbers without context don't drive action.** The dashboard pairs displacement data with AI advancement milestones, showing the direct relationship between new model releases and workforce changes. It also pairs the data with learning resources, so the response to "this is happening" isn't paralysis — it's preparation.

**Transparency builds trust.** Every event is verified against direct company statements, earnings calls, or credible reporting. Events are cross-referenced with research (including Anthropic's labor market impact analysis) to distinguish genuine AI-driven displacement from pandemic-era over-hiring corrections. The methodology is visible. The sources are linked.

**Free means accessible.** Hosted on GitHub Pages with no infrastructure costs. No account required. No paywall. No tracking. The data and code are open source, so anyone can verify, contribute, or build on it.

**The pace of change is accelerating.** In 2023, AI displacement was a handful of cautious announcements. By early 2026, companies like Block (40% of workforce), Amazon (30,000 jobs), and Salesforce (4,000 support roles) are making AI-driven cuts at a scale that demands visibility. This dashboard makes that acceleration impossible to ignore — and provides a path forward for the people affected.

# Meta AI timeline (Key AI models + tools/services → March 17, 2026)

A verified, date-based timeline of Meta’s major AI models and AI products/tools/services (Meta AI assistant, Llama family, AI Studio, Ray-Ban Meta AI features, Segment Anything, Emu, etc.).

## Timeline

| Date (UTC) | What shipped | Category | Notes / what changed |
|---|---|---|---|
| May 3, 2022 | OPT-175B (Open Pretrained Transformer) | Model | Meta shares OPT-175B and related tooling/logbook as a large open language model milestone. |
| Feb 24, 2023 | LLaMA (Llama 1) announced | Model | First Llama family release (research access / weights gated initially). |
| Apr 5, 2023 | Segment Anything (SAM) introduced | Model / Research | “Segment Anything” project released (foundation model + dataset + promptable segmentation concept). |
| Jul 18, 2023 | Llama 2 released (open for research + commercial use) | Model | Meta + Microsoft announce Llama 2 weights and chat-tuned variants with a more permissive license than Llama 1. |
| Aug 24, 2023 | Code Llama released | Model | Code-specialized Llama 2 variants for code generation and discussion; research + commercial use. |
| Sep 27, 2023 | Meta AI assistant + AI characters + AI creative tools announced | Product | Meta AI assistant introduced across WhatsApp/Messenger/Instagram (and “coming soon” to devices), plus AI characters and Emu-based creative tools (e.g., image generation, AI stickers). |
| Nov 16, 2023 | Emu Video + Emu Edit (research) | Model / Research | Meta publishes Emu Video (text-to-video) and Emu Edit (instruction-based image editing) research update. |
| Apr 18, 2024 | Meta Llama 3 released (8B, 70B) | Model | Llama 3 family introduced as a major upgrade; open availability positioning. |
| Apr 18, 2024 | Meta AI “built with Llama 3” announced | Product | Meta AI positioned as the flagship assistant experience built on Llama 3 and integrated across Meta apps. |
| Jul 23, 2024 | Llama 3.1 released (8B, 70B, 405B) | Model | Llama 3.1 introduced, including the 405B model; positioned as “frontier-level” open model. |
| Jul 23, 2024 | Meta AI expands (multilingual + creative updates) | Product | Meta AI expands to more languages/countries; adds/improves creative tooling and optionally uses the largest Llama model for harder tasks. |
| Jul 29, 2024 | AI Studio announced (US rollout begins) | Product / Creator tool | AI Studio introduced so creators and users can build/share custom AI characters; described as built with Llama 3.1. |
| Jul 29, 2024 | SAM 2 (Segment Anything Model 2) released | Model | Next-gen SAM expands promptable segmentation to images + video, tracking objects across frames in real time. |
| Sep 25, 2024 | Llama 3.2 released | Model | Llama 3.2 introduces vision-capable models (11B, 90B) plus lightweight text-only models (1B, 3B) aimed at edge/mobile. |
| Sep 25, 2024 | Ray-Ban Meta glasses get new Meta AI features | Product / Device | New AI features and partner integrations announced (including memory/recall-style help and live translation features depending on rollout/region). |
| Dec 6, 2024 | Llama 3.3 70B released | Model | Text-only model positioned as much more efficient while approaching top-tier performance for its class. |
| Dec 19, 2024 | “The future of AI: Built with Llama” recap post | Company / Platform | Meta recaps the year’s Llama trajectory (including Llama 3.2 and Llama 3.3) and positions open models as the strategy. |
| Apr 5, 2025 | Llama 4 released (Scout + Maverick; Behemoth previewed) | Model | Llama 4 models introduced as natively multimodal open-weight models; larger “Behemoth” referenced as a preview/training foundation. |
| Apr 29, 2025 | First LlamaCon + Meta AI app launched | Product / Platform | Meta announces the first Meta AI app (standalone access + Discover feed) and holds its first LlamaCon developer event. |
| Mar 12, 2026 | Meta delays next flagship model “Avocado” (reporting) | Roadmap (reported) | Reporting indicates Meta pushed the rollout of “Avocado” to at least May 2026 due to performance concerns. |

## Notes on scope
- This timeline focuses on major named launches (models + headline product/platform features).
- Meta ships frequent incremental updates (especially inside Meta AI experiences across WhatsApp/Instagram/Messenger and device rollouts). If you want a “release-notes level” timeline, I can expand it into a longer, more granular list.

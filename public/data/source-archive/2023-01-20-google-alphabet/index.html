Alphabet cuts 12,000 jobs after pandemic hiring spree, refocuses on AI

Published: 2023-01-20

January 20 (Reuters) - Alphabet Inc., the parent company of Google, announced plans to eliminate approximately 12,000 positions as it navigates "a different economic reality," according to a memo sent to staff. The company is prioritizing advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) while reducing roles associated with experimental initiatives.

This reduction represents about 6% of Alphabet's workforce and follows significant layoffs at other major technology firms, including Amazon.com Inc., Microsoft Corp., and Meta Platforms Inc. These companies are scaling back after an expansion during the pandemic led to excess staffing in a challenging economic climate.

Shares of Alphabet experienced a 4% increase on Friday, despite having dropped 30% over the last year, mirroring a 24% decline across the broader tech sector. The company had expanded its workforce by nearly a third during 2020 and 2021.

Sundar Pichai, who has led Alphabet since 2019, expressed in the memo that he accepts "full responsibility" for the choices that resulted in the layoffs. Pichai emphasized the need to "sharpen our focus, reengineer our cost base, direct our talent and capital to our highest priorities."

As a longstanding leader in AI, Alphabet is now contending with increased competition from Microsoft, which is reportedly aiming to enhance its investment in ChatGPT.

Meanwhile, Alphabet's primary revenue stream from advertising is under pressure as businesses cut budgets. Analyst Susannah Streeter from Hargreaves Lansdown remarked, "It's evident that Alphabet is not shielded from the challenging economic environment, with growing concerns regarding a potential U.S. recession."

With Alphabet's recent job reductions, the total layoffs among four of the largest U.S. tech firms have reached 51,000 in recent months. Economist Stuart Cole from Equiti Capital stated, "The tech industry serves as a canary in the coal mine."

Alphabet is preparing for a significant AI launch, with The New York Times reporting that Google intends to introduce over 20 new products and a search engine featuring chatbot capabilities.

Among those affected by the job cuts are recruiters, corporate personnel, and members of engineering and product teams. The majority of layoffs occurred within Area 120, Alphabet's internal incubator for new projects.

In the United States, affected employees will receive severance packages, six months of healthcare coverage, and immigration assistance.

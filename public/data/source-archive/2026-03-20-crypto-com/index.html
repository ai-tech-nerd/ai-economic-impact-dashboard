Crypto.com lays off 12% of workforce as latest company to cite AI in job cuts

Published: 2026-03-19

Crypto.com has announced a reduction of 12% of its workforce as the company adopts artificial intelligence technologies. CEO Kris Marszalek indicated that the layoffs were aimed at "positions that do not adjust to our evolving landscape."

An increasing number of firms have referenced AI as a reason for significant layoffs, as executives reassess the types of roles necessary within their organizations.

On Thursday, the cryptocurrency trading platform Crypto.com revealed its decision to downsize 12% of its employees in light of its integration of artificial intelligence. "We are joining the ranks of companies implementing enterprise-wide AI. Those that fail to make this transition promptly will not survive," CEO Kris Marszalek shared via a post on X.

A representative from Crypto.com informed CNBC that all affected employees have been notified, though they did not disclose the precise number of job cuts.

These layoffs at Crypto.com occur alongside a trend where numerous companies are citing AI as a reason for large-scale workforce reductions. Just last month, Block eliminated over 4,000 positions, nearly half of its total staff. CEO Jack Dorsey communicated to shareholders that "the fundamental idea is straightforward. Intelligent tools have transformed the way a company is built and managed."

Earlier this week, Reuters reported that Meta is contemplating layoffs that could impact as much as 20% of its workforce. This decision is intended to mitigate the company's substantial expenses related to AI infrastructure.

Atlassian, a software company based in Sydney, announced last week that it will be reducing its workforce by 10%, which translates to about 1,600 roles. CEO Mike Cannon-Brookes noted that the layoffs aim to "self-fund additional investments in AI and enterprise sales."

In 2022, Crypto.com laid off 20% of its global workforce due to the downfall of the crypto firm FTX and a "commitment to prudent financial management."

TITLE: AI was behind over 50,000 layoffs in 2025 — here are the top firms to cite it for job cuts
PUBLISHED: 2025-12-21
================================================================================

Layoffs have become a prominent aspect of the employment landscape in 2025, with numerous significant corporations revealing plans for extensive job reductions influenced by advancements in artificial intelligence. According to the consulting firm Challenger, Gray & Christmas, AI accounted for nearly 55,000 job eliminations in the United States this year.

Overall, there were approximately 1.17 million job cuts reported throughout 2025, marking the highest figure since the Covid-19 pandemic in 2020, which saw 2.2 million layoffs announced by year-end.

As inflation continues to exert pressure, tariffs are escalating costs, and companies are seeking to implement cost-saving strategies, AI has emerged as an appealing short-term remedy. A study released by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in November indicated that AI is already capable of performing tasks equivalent to 11.7% of the U.S workforce, potentially saving up to $1.2 trillion in wages across sectors such as finance, healthcare, and various professional services.

However, not everyone is convinced that AI is the sole factor behind the significant layoffs. Fabian Stephany, an assistant professor specializing in AI and labor at the Oxford Internet Institute, previously expressed to CNBC that this could be a convenient excuse. He noted that many organizations that thrived during the pandemic "significantly overhired," suggesting that the recent reductions might merely serve as a "market correction."

Here are some of the leading companies that have identified AI as a factor in their layoff and restructuring strategies for 2025.

In October, Amazon revealed its largest layoff round in history, cutting 14,000 corporate positions. Beth Galetti, Amazon's senior vice president, noted in a blog post: "This generation of AI represents the most transformative technology we've encountered since the Internet, enabling companies to innovate at unprecedented speed... We believe that a leaner organization, with fewer layers and more responsibility, is necessary for us to act swiftly for our customers and our business."

Microsoft has announced a total reduction of approximately 15,000 jobs throughout 2025, with its latest round of cuts in July involving 9,000 positions. CEO Satya Nadella communicated in a memo to staff the company's transition from "a software factory to an intelligence engine."

In September, Salesforce's CEO Marc Benioff confirmed that the firm had cut 4,000 customer support positions with the assistance of AI. "I reduced it from 9,000 heads to about 5,000, as I require fewer personnel."

IBM's CEO Arvind Krishna informed Wall Street in May that AI had taken over the roles of several hundred human resources employees. In November, IBM announced a 1% global reduction that could affect nearly 3,000 workers.

Cybersecurity firm CrowdStrike stated in May that it would lay off 5% of its workforce, equating to 500 employees, directly linking these reductions to AI.

In February, the HR platform Workday was among the first companies this year to announce that it would reduce its workforce by 8.5%, totaling around 1,750 jobs, as it shifts its focus towards investing in AI.

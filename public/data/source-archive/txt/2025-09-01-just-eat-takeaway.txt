A growing number of companies have cited artificial intelligence and automation as reasons for cutting staff in recent months.

In many cases, executives said AI systems were already replacing key roles and allowing teams to operate with far fewer people.

We reviewed hundreds of company disclosures, earnings calls, and major media reporting to identify layoffs where AI or automation was directly named as a driver.

Key Findings:
- Over 100,000 employees were impacted by AI-driven layoffs in 2025.
- Over 30,000 employees have already been impacted by AI-driven layoffs in 2026.
- 45+ CEOs have announced layoffs due to efficiencies made by AI.
- AI layoff announcements came from non-tech industries like finance, logistics, consulting, media, retail, and manufacturing.
- At least 8 companies announced AI-related layoffs affecting 10,000+ employees each (including Accenture, Amazon, Citigroup, Dell, Intel, Microsoft, TCS, and UPS).
- More than 60% of announced layoffs occurred at companies with over 100,000 employees.

## Major AI-Related Layoffs and Hiring Freezes in 2025/2026

No. of Employees Impacted: ~11,000 | Industry: IT Consulting | Date: December 2025
Accenture said it would cut roughly 11,000 roles as part of a restructuring tied to how work is changing inside the firm. CEO Julie Sweet made clear that "those we cannot reskill will be exited", instead focusing on automation and AI tools for internal tasks.

No. of Employees Impacted: ~30,000 | Industry: Technology / E-commerce | Date: October 2025, January 2026

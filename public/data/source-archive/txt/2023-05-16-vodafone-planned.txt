TITLE: New Vodafone boss takes aim at costs with 11,000 global job cuts
PUBLISHED: 2023-05-16
================================================================================

LONDON, May 16 (Reuters) - Vodafone's new CEO, Margherita Della Valle, announced plans to eliminate 11,000 positions globally over the next three years. This decision aims to assist the telecom company in regaining its competitive standing after it cautioned that disappointing results in its largest market, Germany, would adversely affect cash flow.

Shares of Vodafone dipped to their lowest point since 2002, experiencing a 9% decline by the afternoon.

These job reductions mark the largest in Vodafone's history, which currently employs 90,000 individuals across Europe and Africa. Della Valle assumed the role of CEO last month, transitioning from her previous position as CFO, with the objective of revitalizing the company.

"To achieve consistent results, Vodafone needs to transform," she stated. "My main focuses are on customers, simplicity, and growth."

At the beginning of the year, Della Valle aimed at streamlining Vodafone's central operations, initiating 500 job reductions. The company previously announced that it would cut 1,300 jobs in Germany and 1,000 in Italy.

The additional layoffs revealed on Tuesday will affect various European markets and include further reductions in central operations.

The drop in share price on Tuesday stemmed from the company's projected cash flow of 3.3 billion euros for the current financial year, a decrease from 4.8 billion euros for the year ending March 2023. Analysts had anticipated a figure of 3.6 billion euros. Della Valle attributed this downward revision to the timing of cable TV payments in Germany, influenced by a recent legislative change.

Activist investors and competitors have characterized the British firm as cumbersome and slow to adapt to market dynamics. The Emirati telecom company Etisalat has acquired a 14.6% stake, while French telecom tycoon Xavier Niel and Liberty Global are also stakeholders.

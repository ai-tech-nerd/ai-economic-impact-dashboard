TITLE: Chegg to lay off 22% of workforce as AI tools shake up education tech industry
PUBLISHED: 2025-05-12
================================================================================

On Monday, Chegg announced plans to reduce its workforce by approximately 22%, equating to 248 positions, as part of efforts to minimize expenses and optimize its operations. This move comes as students increasingly favor AI-driven tools, such as ChatGPT, over conventional educational technology platforms.

The online education company, which provides services including textbook rentals, tutoring, and homework assistance, has been facing a downturn in website traffic for several months. It cautioned that this downward trend may continue to deteriorate before any signs of recovery emerge.

According to Chegg, Google's enhancement of AI Overviews is restricting web traffic to its own search ecosystem while progressively redirecting inquiries to its Gemini AI platform. The company also noted that various AI firms, including OpenAI and Anthropic, are attracting educators by offering complimentary access to their subscriptions.

As part of the restructuring plan revealed on Monday, Chegg will close its offices in the U.S. and Canada by year-end, alongside efforts to cut back on marketing, product development, and general administrative expenditures.

The anticipated costs from this restructuring are projected to range between $34 million and $38 million, primarily expected to occur during the second and third quarters.

Chegg forecasts that the restructuring will yield cost reductions between $45 million and $55 million in 2025, with expectations of savings reaching $100 million to $110 million by 2026.

Additionally, the company reported its first-quarter results on Monday, indicating a 31% decrease in subscribers, bringing the total to 3.2 million. Revenue also fell by 30%, down to $121 million, with subscription service revenue dropping nearly one-third to $108 million.

In February, Chegg initiated legal action against Google, alleging that the tech giant's search engine was diminishing the demand for original content and undermining the competitive capabilities of publishers through its AI-generated overviews.

As of December 31, Chegg employed 1,271 individuals.

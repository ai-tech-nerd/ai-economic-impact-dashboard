TITLE: Sports Illustrated Makes Mass Layoff of Editorial Staffers, Throwing Its Future Into Question
PUBLISHED: 2024-01-19
================================================================================

UPDATED: Sports Illustrated has faced significant layoffs, severely impacting its editorial team at the nearly 70-year-old sports media outlet.

The union representing Sports Illustrated's editorial staff, associated with the NewsGuild of New York, announced on X that employees were informed on Friday that the Arena Group, its parent organization, "is preparing to terminate a considerable number, potentially all, of the Guild-represented employees at SI due to Authentic Brands Group revoking Arena Group's right to publish SI."

In 2019, Authentic Brands Group acquired Sports Illustrated from Meredith for $110 million. ABG granted publishing rights for the brand to Arena Group (formerly The Maven), which rebranded to Arena Group in 2021. Recently, after Arena Group failed to make a scheduled payment for the SI publishing rights, ABG canceled the license.

On January 2, 2024, the Arena Group "failed to make a quarterly payment" of approximately $3.75 million to ABG. On January 18, ABG informed Arena Group of its decision to terminate the agreement, effective immediately. Following the agreement's termination, Arena Group is liable for a fee of $45 million to ABG.

In a press release dated January 18, the Arena Group announced a "significant reduction" of over 100 employees. The company revealed it is in active negotiations with Bridge Media, which would entail a "substantial investment."

According to the SEC filing by Arena Group, the layoffs of over 100 employees accounted for roughly 33% of its current workforce. The company anticipates incurring total restructuring charges between $5 million to $7 million, predominantly related to employee severance and other termination benefits.

Recent headlines concerning Arena Group's management of Sports Illustrated arose after the site published product reviews attributed to seemingly nonexistent authors with AI-generated images. Following the revelation of these fictitious writers last November, Arena Group announced it would end its partnership with AdVon, the company responsible for the posts.

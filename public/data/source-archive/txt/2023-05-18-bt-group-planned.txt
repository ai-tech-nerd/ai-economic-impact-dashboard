TITLE: BT to cut up to 55,000 jobs by 2030 as fibre and AI arrive
PUBLISHED: 2023-05-18
================================================================================

LONDON, May 18 (Reuters) - BT Group, Britain's biggest broadband and mobile provider, will cut up to 55,000 jobs including contractors by 2030 — potentially over 40% of its workforce — as it completes its fibre roll-out and adapts to new technologies such as AI.

Under the leadership of CEO Philip Jansen, the firm has been implementing a transformation strategy to establish a national fibre infrastructure while also launching high-speed 5G mobile services. On Thursday, the former state-owned enterprise announced growth in revenue and core earnings for the first time in six years for the fiscal year ending in March.

Jansen indicated that after completing the fibre deployment, digitizing operations, embracing AI, and simplifying its organizational structure, BT would function with a significantly smaller workforce and a reduced cost structure by the end of the decade. "The new BT Group will be a more streamlined entity with a promising future," he stated.

The workforce is projected to decrease from 130,000 to between 75,000 and 90,000 by the end of the 2030 financial year, with approximately 30,000 of the current employees being contractors. Jansen noted that job reductions would accelerate as the company wraps up its fibre rollout and phases out 3G services.

The Communication Workers Union (CWU) remarked that the job losses were to be expected given the impending shifts in technology and infrastructure.

In a related move, BT's competitor Vodafone announced on Tuesday that it would eliminate 11,000 jobs globally to regain its competitive stance. Jansen explained that approximately 10,000 fewer network engineers would be required for digital networks, with automation and AI technologies expected to replace another 10,000 roles. He highlighted the "vast opportunities" presented by AI, suggesting that advancements in generative AI large language models could be as transformative as the introduction of smartphones.

BT aims to leverage AI to enhance customer service tailored to client needs. "We won't create an environment where customers feel like they're interacting with a robot," Jansen added.

Regarding the annual results, BT met market expectations with a 5% increase in adjusted core earnings, reaching 7.9 billion pounds ($10 billion). However, free cash flow decreased by 5% to 1.3 billion pounds, falling at the lower end of its forecast due to heightened capital expenditure.

Openreach, BT's network division, reaffirmed its ambition to provide ultra-fast full-fibre connections to 25 million premises by the close of 2026.

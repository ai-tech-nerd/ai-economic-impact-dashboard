UPS announces 12,000 job cuts, says package volume slipped last quarter

Published: 2024-01-30

On Tuesday, UPS announced that it did not meet Wall Street's revenue expectations, revealing a decline in shipping volume both internationally and domestically in its fourth-quarter earnings report. The firm also disclosed plans to cut 12,000 jobs as part of its strategy to optimize resources for 2024.

According to CEO Carol Tomé during a conference call regarding the earnings, these workforce reductions are anticipated to generate approximately $1 billion in savings.

"2023 was an unusual year, and honestly, it was challenging and disappointing. We faced reductions in volume, revenue, and operating profits across all three segments of our business," Tomé stated.

As a result, shares of the logistics giant fell by over 8%.

In terms of performance against Wall Street forecasts, UPS reported net income of $1.4 billion, $1.87 per share, for the final quarter of 2023, down from $3.0 billion, $3.96 per share, the previous year. When adjusting for one-off expenses linked to pensions and intangible assets, UPS's earnings were $2.47 per share.

The company experienced a revenue decline of 7.8%, dropping to $24.9 billion from $27 billion last year. It also saw a 7.4% reduction in average daily volume domestically and an 8.3% decrease internationally. Tomé pointed out that the international downturn was "heavily weighted in Europe along with freight challenges in the Red Sea area and the Panama and Suez canals."

Although the earnings report did not explicitly address any financial repercussions from the August negotiations with the Teamsters regarding labor contracts, Tomé mentioned that these discussions, along with the broader macroeconomic climate, contributed to the "disappointing" results for the year.

Additionally, the company is contemplating the sale of its Coyote truck brokerage division, which Tomé described as a "highly cyclical" operation with "significant earnings volatility." She also indicated that the company intends to require employees return to office five days a week in 2024.

Looking ahead, UPS's forecast for 2024 anticipates revenue between $92 billion and $94.5 billion, with an adjusted operating margin projected to be around 10% to 10.6%.

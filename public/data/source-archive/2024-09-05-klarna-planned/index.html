Are Klarna's AI-led job cuts a model for the payments industry?

Published: 2024-09-05

Last week, Klarna outlined significant financial gains from its recent AI adoption, including a sharp cut in headcount that the company plans to continue. But should others in payments be looking to follow suit?

At the end of August, Swedish buy now, pay later (BNPL) major Klarna released H1 2024 results that showed the company seeing significant operational and financial gains from its recent use of AI.

Through a variety of company-wide initiatives enabled by its partnership with ChatGPT maker OpenAI, the company has seen its number of full-time-equivalent employees drop by 24% within a year, from 5,000 to 3,800. It now plans to go further, with a target of 2,000.

These cuts have enabled it to return to breakeven after several years of climbing losses. Such a focus on returning to profitability is largely driven by a long-anticipated IPO.

Why Klarna needed to lean into AI

Klarna has in the past been a profitable company. It reported profitable yearly results between 2007 and 2018, with its earnings before tax peaking at SEK 523m ($61m) in 2017.

However, the company has recently seen its spending climb as it has sought to significantly broaden its market reach. In May 2022, the company announced that it was laying off 10% of its staff — reportedly around 650 people — and in June 2022, Klarna raised $800m at a valuation of $6.7bn — an 85% reduction from the previous year's high of $45.6bn.

Klarna's AI-led job cuts and hiring freeze

The company's full-time employee (FTEs) headcount is now at 3,800 — only slightly above 2020 levels and around 40% below reported mid-2022 highs of 6,500.

While back in 2022 the company achieved job cuts through active redundancies, over the past year it has shed around 1,200 members of its workforce through natural attrition. Klarna says it has had a hiring freeze in place for all roles except those in engineering.

Speaking to the Financial Times shortly after the release of its H1 24 results, Klarna CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski said: "Not only can we do more with less, but we can do much more with less. Internally, we speak directionally about 2,000 [employees]."

How AI has impacted Klarna's financial performance

In its H1 2024 earnings report, Klarna reported a 27% rise in revenue, a 16% rise in GMV and a 22% increase in gross profit. Revenue per employee in H1 2024 reached SEK 7m ($820,000), a 75% increase on H1 23.

The company reported adjusted operating income of SEK 673m ($79m) in H1 24, a 250% YoY increase.

Klarna's AI initiatives in detail

Klarna became an early partner of OpenAI when it launched ChatGPT-based shopping in March 2023. At the start of 2024, Klarna launched its AI assistant built into the Klarna app. One month after launch, Klarna announced that the AI assistant was doing the same work as 700 full-time human agents, having handled two-thirds of the company's customer service chats. Average time to resolve had dropped from 11 minutes to 2 minutes.

The company now projects that its AI assistant will drive $40m in profit improvements across 2024.

90% of all employees are using OpenAI-based GenAI tools on a daily basis.

Drawbacks and challenges

An analysis of Klarna's Glassdoor rating shows a notable drop in average rating among Klarna employees between 2022 (3.8) and 2024 (3.0). Multiple reviews cited high workloads and stress levels, with at least two specifically raising concerns around burnout.

Beyond employees, there are also risks associated with brand perception when it comes to being so vocal about using AI to cut headcount.

AI initiatives across the payments industry

Our analysis of the discussion of AI by 16 different publicly traded cross-border payments companies across their financial 2021-2023 earnings calls showed that while less than half (44%) discussed the technology at all in 2021, by 2023 this had risen to 75%.

For companies looking to increase their use of AI, Klarna provides a showcase of what is possible with high levels of commitments to the technology. However, such speed has come at the cost of employee satisfaction. For those not on Klarna's deadline, there may be a case for exercising some caution.

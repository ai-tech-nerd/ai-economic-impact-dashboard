Duolingo lays off staff as language learning app shifts toward AI

Published: 2024-01-09

Duolingo has announced that it has reduced its contract workforce by roughly 10%, as stated in a report to CNN on Tuesday. This decision aligns with the company's strategy to increasingly integrate artificial intelligence into its operations.

Although not all of the job cuts were directly linked to technological advancements, Duolingo terminated some contractor positions at the end of 2023 to facilitate AI-driven modifications in content creation and distribution. The firm clarified that no full-time employees were impacted by these layoffs and that efforts were made to find alternative positions for those affected before resorting to 'off-boarding' as a final measure.

As a virtual language tutor, Duolingo boasts 24.2 million daily users, 5.8 million paid subscribers, and offers over 100 different courses.

In its commitment to incorporating AI into its services, Duolingo introduced a new subscription level called 'Duolingo Max' in March. This tier utilizes OpenAI's sophisticated language model GPT-4, enabling features such as engaging in full conversations with a chatbot for skill practice and receiving AI-generated clarifications regarding the correctness of answers.

"Generative AI is speeding up our processes by allowing us to produce new content significantly faster," stated CEO Luis von Ahn in a letter to shareholders in November.

In light of the contractor layoffs, Duolingo indicated that AI will play a larger role in tasks like generating sentences for courses, compiling lists of valid translations, and analyzing user error reports to rectify mistakes more efficiently.

Despite reducing its workforce to leverage AI for content creation and verification, Duolingo emphasized that human oversight is still essential for reviewing AI-generated work. "We are not replacing the knowledge of human specialists with AI," the company explained to CNN. "AI serves as a tool to enhance productivity and efficiency."

Duolingo is not the only organization making workforce reductions in favor of AI. A report from ResumeBuilder in November revealed that 37% of surveyed companies indicated they had replaced employees with AI in 2023. Furthermore, 44% anticipated that this technology could lead to additional layoffs in 2024.

Additionally, in late April, Dropbox announced it would be reducing its workforce by about 16%, equating to around 500 positions, also citing AI as a factor in its decision-making process.

This report was contributed by CNN's Catherine Thorbecke.
